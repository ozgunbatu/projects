Mobile App UX Research – Banking Flow

Drop-offs were observed during identity verification and onboarding. User interviews and journey mapping were conducted, and Figma prototypes were tested with 42 participants. UX improvements were derived to enhance completion rates and user satisfaction.

Data sources: Figma Prototype

Problem Definition

High drop-off rates were observed during user identity verification and onboarding processes. Completion rates were inconsistent, and user frustration was recorded in qualitative interviews.

Data & Methodology

  • User journeys were mapped and analyzed to detect friction points.
  • Figma wireframes and interactive prototypes were tested with 42 participants.
  • Qualitative feedback was coded to identify recurring pain points.
  • Completion metrics and drop-off rates were measured and compared before and after UX interventions.

Recommendations

  • Identity verification steps were simplified and inline validation was implemented.
  • Progress indicators and clear error feedback were incorporated.
  • Prototype iterations were used to refine onboarding flows.
  • User testing was scheduled continuously to validate improvements.

Results

  • Drop-off rates were reduced by 18%
  • Completion rates increased by 20%
  • Positive usability feedback was recorded from all participants
