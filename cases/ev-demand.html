EV Demand Forecast – Germany

← Back to Portfolio

Germany’s electric vehicle adoption is rising rapidly, creating regional imbalances in inventory. Some regions experienced stock surpluses, while others faced shortages, increasing operational costs and slowing delivery times. Using historical sales and registration data, we analyzed and optimized inventory allocation, improved forecast accuracy, and enabled faster data-driven decisions.

Problem

For example, Hamburg had 20 vehicles in stock while demand was 50, causing extra logistics cost. Munich faced shortages as demand exceeded supply. Forecasting errors caused slow replenishment and operational inefficiencies.

Approach

  • Collected 2.3M rows of historical sales and registration data.
  • Cleaned and normalized datasets in SQL (removed duplicates, null handling).
  • Analyzed regional demand patterns using Python and Power BI dashboards.
  • Simulated inventory allocation per region to minimize overstock and shortages.

Recommendation

Implement region-specific inventory dashboards with weekly updates. Optimize stock allocation using forecast model outputs to reduce costs, improve delivery times, and increase operational efficiency.
