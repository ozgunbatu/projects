Warehouse Optimization – Logistic Operations

Applying Lean & Six Sigma principles to optimize warehouse operations, reduce inventory costs, and improve throughput efficiency.

Problem

High operational costs and delayed order fulfillment due to inefficient warehouse layout, redundant picking paths, and uneven inventory distribution across regions.

Approach

Collected 1.5M transaction records from warehouse management systems (WMS) and ERP logs. Conducted:

  • Process mapping of inbound/outbound flows
  • Time-motion study of picking & packing
  • Six Sigma DMAIC methodology to identify bottlenecks
  • Lean 5S assessment for warehouse layout
  • Simulation modeling of inventory allocation per SKU and region

Recommendation

Redesign warehouse layout based on ABC analysis, implement batch picking for high-volume SKUs, standardize processes using Lean 5S, and apply automated alert system for low-stock items.

Implementation

  • Power BI dashboard for real-time inventory monitoring with regional heatmap
  • Simulation of picking routes to minimize travel distance
  • SQL queries automated for weekly performance reports
  • KPI cards for throughput, picking accuracy, and inventory turnover

Throughput ↑15%

Inventory Cost ↓12%

Picking Accuracy ↑10%

Order Lead Time ↓20%

Results

After implementation, warehouse efficiency improved significantly:

  • Optimized stock allocation reduced overstock in low-demand regions
  • Picking route optimization reduced operator travel time by 18%
  • Lean process standardization decreased operational errors
  • Real-time dashboard enabled proactive decision-making by operations team

Data sources: Kaggle warehouse & logistics datasets, internal WMS & ERP logs.
